Step 2: Expand the Brushes view in Gimp, Refresh Brushes

If the brush dialog is not open, you can open by Shift+Ctrl+B on your keyboard (Windows). You will want to expand the Brushes view. Also it is a good idea to refresh the brushes. All the brushes install in step 1 should be there now.

Step 3: Expand your canvas to match the size of your mockup

The canvas is where you will be dropping the brushes to create your mockup

Step 4: Drag your first brush from the brush window to the canvas

Pick one of the browser screen brushes (size to use should be less than the size of your canvas) and drag it onto your canvas. Each brush you drop onto the canvas this way will create a new layer for the brush that is the same size as the brush.

Step 5: Create your guides

I suggest splitting the usuable screen into 9 sections.

Step 6: Drag each additional brush you require onto your canvas

Continue dragging brushes to your specified location for each element you want on your mockup

Step 7: Save your mockup as a Gimp .xcf file then save a copy as jpg,png, or other image format

Saving your file as a Gimp .xcf will enable you to make changes to the template later which can then be save a copy as image to share with your collabarators.
